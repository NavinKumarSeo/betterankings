ChatGPT SEO: how to get your brand recommended by ChatGPT

When a buyer asks ChatGPT "what's the best [your category] tool?", it answers with a shortlist of brands — by name. If you're not on that list, you've lost the deal before a human ever compared features. ChatGPT SEO is the work of getting onto that list. Here's how ChatGPT actually decides, and what moves the needle.

The mechanics

How ChatGPT decides which brands to name

It isn't random, and it isn't your Google ranking. Three inputs drive it:

01

Training data

How widely and consistently your brand is described as a category option across the web the model learned from. Mentioned everywhere, the same way? You get named. Invisible or inconsistent? You don't.

02

Live browsing

For current or specific queries, ChatGPT searches the web and reads results in real time — so what ranks and what's freshly published about you matters right now, not just in the training set.

03

Trusted sources

It leans on the review aggregators, comparison articles, and communities it treats as authoritative for your category. If competitors own those sources and you don't, they get cited and you get skipped.

What actually moves it

Getting named in ChatGPT, concretely

None of this happens by buying ads or chasing a single keyword. ChatGPT visibility is earned by changing what the web says about you and how machines can parse it:

  • Consistent category description — be defined the same way ("X is a [category] for [audience]") everywhere you appear
  • Machine-readable pages — clear structure and schema so models can extract what you do, who for, and why
  • Presence on cited sources — the review sites, "best of" comparisons and threads ChatGPT pulls from
  • Entity clarity — an unambiguous, well-linked brand identity the model can resolve confidently
